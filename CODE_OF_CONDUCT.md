# Code of conduct

TraceWhy is built in public and expects respectful, technically honest collaboration.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate, specific, and patient with people of different backgrounds and experience levels.
- Critique code, evidence, and decisions without attacking the person presenting them.
- Protect private trace data and immediately call out accidental disclosure.
- Disclose conflicts of interest and distinguish verified behavior from assumptions.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about scope, safety, and evidence quality without harassment.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, doxxing, deliberate exposure of private data, sustained disruption, and retaliation are not tolerated in project spaces or when representing the project.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the maintainer through the repository owner's GitHub profile. Include links or screenshots when safe. Reports will be reviewed confidentially and in good faith. Maintainers may edit or remove content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban a participant based on severity and pattern.

This policy applies to repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, release channels, and project-related public interactions.
